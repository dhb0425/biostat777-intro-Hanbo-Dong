About

About me

I’m Hanbo Dong, a Biostatistics PhD student at Johns Hopkins University. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, non-parametric modeling, and reproducible pipelines for biomedical data. I enjoy turning complex datasets into clear, decision-ready insights in public health and biology.

Selected experience

  • Virome α-diversity depth correction (2024) — Two-step quantile B-spline (with COBS) that outperforms rarefaction in sims & real data.
  • Structure-led virome annotation (2023–2024) — AlphaFold2 + Foldseek to cluster viral proteins for function-based catalogs.
  • Longitudinal microbiome correction (2023–2024) — Zero-inflated quantile regression models; Bayesian logistic component for separation.
